CONFERENCES

2016

The working title for our 2016 conference is 'At the Crossroads: Religion, Politics and Identity.' It will take place on Saturday, 16 April 2016 from 10 am onwards. We retain the format of last year in having a series of lectures, followed by a concluding panel discussion and dinner. We hope that the combination of inspiring and challenging content as well as the interaction between participants will enable us to fully explore and experience interfaith in action. More information will follow shortly.

2015

The theme of the 2015 conference was 'Conflict and Coexistence: the Breaking and Making of Interfaith Relations.' The aim of the conference was to highlight the underlying reasons for the global fragmentation of interfaith relations, including looking at case studies and theories. Our guests were Professor Louise Richardson, Dr Jeffrey Murer, Dr Jasmine Gani, Shyam Bhatia, Professor Jonathan Spencer and Najib Bajali.

2014

The inaugural conference in 2014 was titled 'Coexistence Conference: Tolerance and Plurality in the Middle East.' The conference drew heavily upon the ample resources of St Andrews to feature representatives including Professor Mario Aguilar, the Director of the Centre for the Study of Religion and Politics, the Reverend Donald MacEwan, the University's Chaplain, Dr Mia Spiro, lecturer in Jewish Studies at the University of Glasgow, Imam Hamza ibn Abdurrahman, the Mosque Leader at the Dundee Central Mosque. Each approached the topic based on his or her particular theological, disciplinary and historical background to bring a diversity of views to St Andrews' hollowed and historic Parliament Hall.
