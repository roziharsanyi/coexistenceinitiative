... on our work

... on our 2014 conference

"It was a pleasure to be involved with the first Coexistence in the Middle East Conference. Not only was I impressed with the quality of contributions, but the very idea of Islamic, Jewish and Christian students sitting down together to devise, plan and organise such an event is a profound statement of hope."

Reverend Doctor Donald McEwan, University Chaplain

"Attending last year's Coexistence Conference was a fantastic experience that really opened my eyes to some of the complex issues facing the Middle East from a more academic perspective, as studying Medicine means that opportunities to hear such renowned lecturers in this field are few and far between."

Sarah Levy, Student graduating in 2015, University of St Andrews

"Although I personally was not born and raised in the Middle East and thus was not familiar with much interfaith coexistence issue that existed there, I still found Coexistence in the Middle East conference very enlightening. This was down to the fact that many speakers from various faith backgrounds gathered and shared their opinions which engendered different perspectives on the topic."

Amsyar Jaafar, Islamic Society, University of St Andrews
